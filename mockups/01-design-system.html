Why vivid orange?

A confident, saturated accent (#E85D04) signals warmth, urgency, and modernity — a deliberate break from the navy + gold pharma convention. Reserved for CTAs and emphasis spans only.

Why electric royal blue?

Pulled directly from the BeCon wordmark — cyan-to-royal gradient. Modern, technological, optimistic. Pairs cleanly with the warm orange accent.

Restraint rule

Orange appears max once per viewport as a CTA, and one emphasis span per heading. If a page can't pass that, it's being overused.

Elevation system

Three shadow tiers (sm / md / lg) and four radii (sm / md / lg / xl). Cards land on md elevation, lift to lg on hover. Buttons stay pill-shaped.

02 / Type

One family. Inter Tight.

Variable weight, tight letter-spacing on display, generous line-height on body. Loaded once via next/font for zero CLS. No serif pairing — the modern grotesque does both the editorial and UI work, the way Sia Partners does it.

08 / Summary

What changes vs. the draft. Three moves.

Vivid orange + electric royal blue

Logo-derived blues (#1F66E8 primary, with the cyan→royal gradient at #4DBEFA → #1426A8) paired with vivid orange (#E85D04). Warm-cool tension carries the brand; orange stays scarce so it always cashes the cheque.

Syne+DM Sans → Inter Tight

One variable family. Calmer rhythm, modern grotesque, closer to Sia Partners than the agency draft.

Raindrop photograph hero

Concrete metaphor (signal as ripples in water) replaces abstract geometry. Image-led hero with restrained type overlay.

Rounder, softer surfaces

Cards at 16px radius with layered subtle shadows. Pill-shaped buttons. Strip cells wrapped in a single rounded container. Less boxy, more contemporary.

Bolder section rhythm

Six bands cycling through paper, white, peach, blue, navy, and ink. Each section feels distinct as you scroll. Restraint rule: never two bold sections back-to-back.
